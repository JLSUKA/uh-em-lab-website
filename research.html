Research

Electromagnetics from first principles to measurement.

Our research combines analytical insight, numerical computation, prototyping, and controlled experiments across a focused set of electromagnetic problems.

01

Antenna systems

Designing structures that shape radiation.

We study antenna concepts across microwave and millimeter-wave frequencies, with attention to physical mechanisms, efficiency, bandwidth, pattern control, and practical realization.

Topics

  • Microstrip antennas
  • Leaky-wave antennas
  • Dielectric resonator antennas

Methods

  • Full-wave simulation
  • Prototype fabrication
  • Pattern and impedance measurement
02

Computational EM

Turning Maxwell's equations into reliable tools.

We develop and apply numerical methods for structures whose geometry, materials, or frequency behavior require more than a closed-form treatment.

Topics

  • Full-wave field modeling
  • Periodic structures
  • High-frequency effects

Methods

  • Numerical formulation
  • Convergence studies
  • High-performance computation
03

Waves & materials

Understanding waves in engineered environments.

We investigate how periodicity, dispersion, resonances, and material response can create useful electromagnetic behavior.

Topics

  • Metamaterials
  • Plasmonics
  • Wave propagation

Methods

  • Dispersion analysis
  • Field interpretation
  • Experiment and validation
