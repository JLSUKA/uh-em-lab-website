About

A rigorous and collaborative research environment.

The group brings together electromagnetic theory, computation, fabrication, and measurement while training students to move confidently across all four.

Our mission

01

Developing ideas, tools, and researchers.

The Electromagnetics Group advances fundamental and applied work in antennas and computational electromagnetics. We pursue questions that benefit from both mathematical depth and physical validation.

Our projects are designed to produce useful knowledge while giving students broad technical ownership: defining a problem, building a model, testing assumptions, and communicating results.

How we work

Research as a complete cycle.

Strong work moves repeatedly between theory and evidence.

01

Understand

Begin with electromagnetic fundamentals and a precise research question.

02

Model

Translate the physics into analytical and numerical tools that can be tested.

03

Build

Fabricate representative structures and prepare meaningful experiments.

04

Validate

Compare measurement, simulation, and theory to refine the explanation.

Student development

Training beyond a single technique.

Students build depth in their research topic while developing skills in technical writing, presentations, teamwork, reproducible computation, laboratory practice, and peer review.
